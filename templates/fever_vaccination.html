🌡️ Fever & Vaccination

Handling Mild Fever After Vaccination

A mild fever after vaccination is common in newborns and infants. It indicates that the immune system is responding. With proper care, your baby can remain comfortable while their body builds protection.

💡 Care at Home:
  • Keep your baby well-hydrated — offer breast milk or formula frequently.
  • Dress your baby in light clothing and avoid overheating.
  • Monitor your baby’s temperature regularly using a reliable thermometer.
  • Provide comfort and gentle cuddles to soothe your baby.
🌸 When to Contact Your Pediatrician:
  • If fever lasts more than 48 hours.
  • If your baby has a temperature above 102°F (38.9°C) or shows signs of unusual discomfort.
  • If there are any other concerning symptoms like rash, persistent vomiting, or lethargy.
⚠️ Important Notes:
  • Fever after vaccination is usually temporary and harmless.
  • Never give medication without consulting your pediatrician.
  • Document any reactions after vaccination to share with your doctor.
