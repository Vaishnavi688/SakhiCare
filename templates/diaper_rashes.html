🩲 Diaper Rashes

Prevent & Soothe Your Baby’s Delicate Skin

Diaper rash is common in newborns and can cause redness, discomfort, or mild irritation. With proper care and preventive measures, you can keep your baby’s diaper area healthy and comfortable.

💡 Prevention Tips:
  • Change diapers frequently — ideally every 2–3 hours or when soiled.
  • Allow some air time — leave the diaper off for a few minutes to let the skin breathe.
  • Use soft, breathable diapers and avoid tight-fitting ones.
  • Wash hands before and after changing to maintain hygiene.
🌸 Soothing & Treatment:
  • Apply a thin layer of zinc oxide cream to protect and soothe irritated skin.
  • Use fragrance-free wipes or lukewarm water for cleaning the diaper area.
  • Avoid harsh soaps or wipes with alcohol that can worsen irritation.
⚠️ When to Consult a Pediatrician:
  • If redness persists beyond a few days despite care.
  • If blisters, pus, or bleeding appear.
  • If your baby shows signs of severe discomfort or fever.
