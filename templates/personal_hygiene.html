Carefree: How to Maintain Personal Hygiene During Periods

💬 “Good hygiene is not just about staying clean — it’s about staying confident, healthy, and comfortable during your period.”

🧼 1️⃣ Change Regularly

Change your sanitary pad or tampon every 4–6 hours — even if the flow is light. For menstrual cups, empty and rinse every 8–10 hours (or as needed).

🩷 Remember: Staying dry and clean keeps infections away.

🩲 2️⃣ Wear Clean & Breathable Underwear

Always wear cotton underwear — it allows air circulation and absorbs sweat. Avoid synthetic or tight clothing that traps moisture. Change underwear daily, especially during heavy flow days.

🩷 Comfort = Confidence.

🚿 3️⃣ Wash the Intimate Area Properly

Wash your private parts twice a day using mild, unscented soap and lukewarm water. Avoid using strong soaps, douches, or sprays. Pat dry gently with a clean towel.

🩷 Tip: Always wash from front to back to prevent infections.

🩸 4️⃣ Dispose of Used Products Safely

Wrap used pads or tampons in paper before discarding — never flush them. Wash and sterilize menstrual cups or cloth pads before storing. Use biodegradable bags for eco-friendly disposal.

🩷 Respect your body, respect the planet.

🌼 5️⃣ Stay Fresh & Comfortable

Keep wet wipes or tissues handy when outside. Carry an extra pad, panty liner, or underwear. Use mild powder or anti-rash cream if needed to prevent irritation.

🩷 Your comfort matters every single day.

💧 6️⃣ Stay Hydrated & Eat Healthy

Drink plenty of water, juices, or herbal tea. Eat iron-rich foods like spinach, beetroot, and jaggery. Avoid too much caffeine or junk food.

🩷 Healthy body = happy period!

🌸 7️⃣ Emotional Hygiene Matters Too

Rest when tired. Talk to friends, sisters, or mentors if you feel low. Practice light yoga, meditation, or deep breathing for mental calmness.

🩷 Caring for your mind is as important as caring for your body.

💬 “Menstrual hygiene is not a luxury — it’s self-respect. Take care of your body, and your body will take care of you.” 🌸
