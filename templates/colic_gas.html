🤱 Colic & Gas in Babies

Gentle Remedies & Tips for Your Newborn

Colic and gas are common in newborns and can make them fussy. With gentle care and proper techniques, you can help your baby feel comfortable and reduce discomfort.

💡 Feeding Tips:
  • Feed your baby slowly and in an upright position.
  • Burp your baby after every feed to release trapped air.
  • Use small, frequent feeds if the baby tends to gulp air quickly.
🌸 Massage & Comfort:
  • Gently massage your baby’s tummy in clockwise circular motions to relieve gas.
  • Hold your baby upright and rock gently to provide comfort.
  • Warm compresses (like a warm towel) on the tummy may help ease discomfort.
⚠️ When to Consult a Pediatrician:
  • If colic persists beyond 3 months of age.
  • If the baby shows signs of severe pain, vomiting, or blood in stools.
  • Always follow your pediatrician’s advice for ongoing concerns.
