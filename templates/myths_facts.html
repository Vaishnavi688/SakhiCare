Carefree: Common Myths & Facts About Periods

💬 “Periods are natural, healthy, and nothing to be ashamed of. Let’s bust the myths and embrace the facts!”

❌ Myth: You shouldn’t wash your hair during periods.
✅ Fact: Washing your hair is completely safe. You can maintain hygiene as usual.
❌ Myth: You shouldn’t take a bath or shower.
✅ Fact: Bathing keeps you fresh and reduces the risk of infection. Warm water can also ease cramps.
❌ Myth: You can’t exercise or play sports.
✅ Fact: Light to moderate exercise can reduce cramps, improve mood, and boost energy. Yoga, walking, and stretching are excellent options.
❌ Myth: Periods are dirty or impure.
✅ Fact: Menstruation is a healthy, natural process — it’s not unhygienic or shameful.
❌ Myth: You shouldn’t eat certain foods (like spicy or sour foods).
✅ Fact: No food is strictly off-limits. Some women prefer to avoid caffeine or very oily foods, but balanced nutrition is important for energy and comfort.
❌ Myth: You can’t enter temples or kitchens during periods.
✅ Fact: Periods do not make you impure. Cultural restrictions are not scientifically necessary. Women can engage in normal activities safely.
❌ Myth: You should hide your period.
✅ Fact: Talking about periods openly is healthy, empowering, and normalizes menstruation for everyone.

💖 “Knowledge is power. Break the myths, embrace your cycle, and feel confident every day.”
