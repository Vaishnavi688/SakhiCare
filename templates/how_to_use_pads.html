Carefree — How to Use Sanitary Pads

💕 Stay Clean. Stay Confident. Be Carefree.

Periods are a natural and powerful part of being a woman — and taking care of yourself during those days helps you feel fresh, comfortable, and confident. Here’s a simple, friendly guide to using sanitary pads safely 🌸

🩸 1️⃣ Choose the Right Pad

Select a pad that fits your flow — light, medium, or heavy.
During the first 2 days, use a pad with extra absorption or wings to avoid leaks.
At night, prefer a long night pad for full protection while you rest.

💡 Tip: Try to keep an extra pad with you when you go out — just in case.

🌸 2️⃣ Wash Your Hands

Always wash your hands with soap and water before and after changing your pad.
This helps keep you clean and prevents infection.

🌼 3️⃣ Unwrap and Place the Pad

Remove the pad from its wrapper gently.

Peel off the strip to expose the sticky side.
Stick the pad firmly in the center of your underwear (the part that touches your body).
If it has wings, fold them around the sides to keep the pad in place.

💡 Tip: Make sure the pad fits comfortably — not too front or back.

🌷 4️⃣ Change Every 4–6 Hours

Don’t keep the same pad for too long. Change it every 4–6 hours, or sooner if it feels wet or uncomfortable.
This prevents rashes, bad odor, and infections.

💧 Remember: Clean yourself with tissue or water before putting on a new pad.

🌿 5️⃣ Dispose Properly

Wrap the used pad in paper or the wrapper it came in.
Always throw it in a dustbin — never flush it in the toilet.
If you can, use a sanitary waste bin for safe disposal.

💡 Tip: Wash your hands again after disposing.

💖 6️⃣ Stay Fresh and Relax

Wear cotton underwear — it allows your skin to breathe.
Drink water, eat fruits, and try light exercises or yoga to ease cramps.
Remember, taking care of yourself is not extra — it’s essential.

💬 “Periods don’t stop you — they remind you how strong and beautiful you truly are.”
